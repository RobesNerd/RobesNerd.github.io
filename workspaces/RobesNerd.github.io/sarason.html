Sarason

Rejected for acting on impulse. Proved herself through sacrifice. She became exactly what her father was, on her own terms.

The Warrior

Sarason is the bravest member of the five spies. Fully armoured, fast on her feet, and lethal with a sword, she is the group's front line in every fight. She does not hesitate. That instinct, the one that got her rejected from the army, is also what saves the mission on Day 3 of the journey.

When everyone else has crossed the River Glin, Sarason stays behind to guard the chest. She is found nearly dead, covered in claw marks, the chest gone. But the battle plans are safe in her hands.

The Daughter

Her father was the greatest general Slinsil ever had. He died in battle when Sarason was still young. Her mother raised her alone with love and quiet strength, and Sarason grew up knowing that her father was a hero and that she wanted to be one too.

The army rejected her for acting on impulse instead of strategy. That rejection never stopped hurting. Everything she does on the mission is, in some quiet way, a proof of what she is capable of.

They got the chest. But they did not get the plans.
